Interest IT

What is your interest in IT?

My interests in IT are software programming, networking, and cyber security. I am interested in IT because it creates a virtual world, and it becomes a reality and coexists with us that we even live.

When did your interest in IT start?

It started when I started playing video and online computer games in my teens. And it once drove me to study IT in school when I was around 16 years old. However, due to the concerns of parents and their expectations of their children, I did not study and therefore, my path to IT has been divided since then.

Was there a particular event or person that sparked your interest? Outline your IT experience (if any).

I would say Steve Jobs and all those IT guys in Sci-Fi or crime and action movies. For the programming side, When I first used Apple products, I was fascinated by their appearance and features. It is different from Microsoft products. It amazed me, and I thought the interface and functions could be attractive, user-friendly and various. For cyber security and networking, I would say that I would always be excited about how they successfully stopped the cyberattacks from those malicious hackers when I see the movies. Not to mention I am interested in hacking skills as well.

Why did you choose to come to RMIT?

I have always wanted to take advanced studies to improve myself by learning a new skill to change my career. However, being in multiple roles in my life and concerned with reality, I had to put this idea of studying aside and wait. And I have known Open University Australia for years, and this year, I think I may be ready to study, and I contacted them, then here I am.

What do you expect to learn during your studies?

I expect to learn the general knowledge of IT at first, and then I can analyse and consider which interests me more and suits me so that I can specialise in one field or two.
